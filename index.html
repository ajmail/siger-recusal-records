Key Findings

1. No InnovatePGH Abstention Memo Exists in the Production
The production contains two formal Abstention Memoranda dated February 9, 2023. One covers the Jewish Federation of Greater Pittsburgh. One covers Carnegie Mellon University. Neither covers InnovatePGH. DCED's denial letter states these memos were used as "authority documents for other non-specifically named recusals." InnovatePGH (EIN 27-1727604) is an independent 501(c)(3). It is not a CMU subsidiary. Siger served as Vice Chair of InnovatePGH's board directly. That conflict is specific to InnovatePGH and is not resolved by a CMU abstention.
"I do have a recusal on file for innovatepgh I believe."
Secretary Rick Siger — Internal DCED Email — April 3, 2026, 6:15 AM
2. Six Senior Officials Coordinated a Four-Sentence Response
Within hours of Matin's April 2 email, Siger convened his Chief Counsel, Communications Director, Executive Deputy Secretary, and two additional senior staff to coordinate a response. The resulting reply was four sentences confirming a recusal that no document in the production supports. You do not deploy six senior officials including legal and communications leadership to answer a routine constituent inquiry.
3. A COI Protocols Document Was Under Development and Withheld
On February 6, 2026, Chief Counsel Irwin emailed Siger referencing a Conflict of Interest Protocols document described as pending presentation to senior DCED staff and department-wide dissemination. The document was withheld as predecisional. A completed policy document ready for department-wide distribution is a public record. Whether it was finalized or abandoned is unresolved. The OOR appeal will seek its production.
4. The Recusal Cannot Create Real Separation
Sean Luther is CEO of InnovatePGH. Sean Luther co-governs Team Pennsylvania alongside Secretary Siger. Team Pennsylvania sets economic development policy that DCED implements. A recusal from InnovatePGH that does not account for this co-governance relationship is structural separation on paper only. The same network governs the policy, the funding, and the intermediaries simultaneously.
